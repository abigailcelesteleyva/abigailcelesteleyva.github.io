Journey

From physical artifacts to AI-native interfaces.

Everlasting Bloom — An aluminum flower lamp I designed and hand made '23

I've always been obsessed with why we interact with the world the way we do. For me, design is the bridge between psychology and problem-solving.

My foundation is in the physical world — designing for "human-scale" challenges where ergonomics and tactile feedback are everything. I still carry those lessons into my digital work; whether I'm refining a dev environment or crafting an AI-native interface, I treat every pixel with the same intentionality as a physical product.

"A well-designed lamp doesn't just light a room; it dictates the mood, the movement, and the utility of the space. Digital design should do the same."

Today, I'm focused on building intuitive, deeply human experiences at the intersection of AI and UX. I believe the best technology shouldn't feel like a tool — it should feel like an extension of the person using it.
